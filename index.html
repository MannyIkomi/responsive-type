The Type of our Time

is a geometric sans serif typeface designed by Paul Renner released in 1927 by the Bauer Type Foundry. Futura is based on geometric shapes like the circle, triangle and squares with restrained optical adjustments. The geometric shapes make this font feel symmetrical, modular, industrial, and modern. All letter forms use the same uniform line weight with minimal contrast. The capital letters have similar proportions to roman square capitals. Unlike other sans serif typefaces at the time, Futura had a relatively low x-height, making it one of the first to be suitable for body copy. Futura is one of the most popular fonts of the twentieth century.

History of Graphic Design

Course Description

This course critically investigates and explores graphic design and visual communication history, major movements and pivotal artists and designers. This course requires participation of students in lecture and presentation, writing and studio projects, which will build critical thinking and visual skills. The course provides a necessary historical basis for students in the Graphic Design Program in the Visual & Media Arts Department. Prerequisites: Writing Skills II (ENG095) and Prealgebra (MAT092).

Required Text

Meggs’ History of Graphic Design, 6th edition
Publisher: Wiley;
ISBN-10: 0471699020
ISBN-13: 978-0471699026

Course Description

Upon successful completion of the course, the student will be able to

  • Identify graphic design trends throughout the history of visual communication and understand the stylistic, social and technological significance of those milestones in history.
  • Be able to use the visual language from each era studied to inspire their own work by applying various styles to independent studio projects.
  • Develop critical thinking skills through reading, writing, drawing and discussing course material,and presenting and critiquing their work and the work of classmates.
  • Develop communication skills through participating in class discussions and critiques.

Required Materials
