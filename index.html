Responsive Typography

As part of my transition from print to user interface I experimented with recreating my type posters and documents. The objective was to re-imagine my static design as a fluid, interactive web page. Along the way I began incorporating, animation, typographic features, and new CSS Grid layout methods. My approach was inspired by a series of projects by Jen Simmons.

For the best viewing experience please use a larger tablet/desktop with a modern browser. All designs have been progressivley enhanced from smaller viewports.

The Type of our Time

is a geometric sans serif typeface designed by Paul Renner, released in 1927 by the Bauer Type Foundry. Futura is based on simple shapes like the circle, triangle and squares with restrained optical adjustments. The geometric shapes make this font feel symmetrical, modular, industrial, and modern. All letter forms use the same uniform line weight with minimal contrast. The capital letters have similar proportions to roman square capitals. Unlike other sans serif typefaces at the time, Futura had a relatively low x‑height, making it one of the first to be suitable for body copy. Futura is one of the most popular typefaces of the twentieth century.
